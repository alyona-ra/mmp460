What's The Lottery?

Housing is considered affordable if it costs less or about one-third of your income. Unfortunately a lot of apartments in NYC are a lot more expensive than that! By applying to the NYC Housing Lottery you’ll be entered into a database of people that qualify for listings of real affordable housing as it becomes available. Those interested in affordable housing can find units according to their household size and income level in all five boroughs. Rent increases are also regulated over time.

The Lottery partners with The New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) and Housing Development Corporation (HDC) who own and monitor the buildings that are part of the program.

Can I Apply?

There are a lot of options for you depending on your household size and income. Find the one that suits you best. Here are the steps you need to follow to apply successfully:

  • Create a profile on housingconnect.nyc.gov.
  • Browse available opportunities.
  • Apply before the application deadline.
  • After applying, make sure to gather your credit or rental history provide even more detailed instructions on how to do that.
  • Don’t forget to save up for the first month’s rent and security deposit.

What Do I Need?

Start collecting copies of your documents today to speed up the application process tomorrow! You will be asked to confirm:

  • Birth certificates, picture IDs of all your household members.
  • Federal or state tax returns
  • Proof of Social Security and any other income of everyone in your household.
  • Your current lease, receipts for rent, recent gas or electric bills – any piece of information about your current apartment.

Keep in mind that you might have to do some follow-up steps to complete your application. Don’t send both online and paper applications as you may be disqualified!
