OUR
MISSION

This campaign was created to help those who seek affordable housing find one. We are not the Housing Lottery, rather a user guide for it. We want you to learn more about the Lottery and explore the options you have. We believe that registration process and future participation in the Lottery should be as easy as they can.

THE
PROBLEM

According to NYTimes, 25 million people applied for 40,000 affordable housing units between 2013 and 2020 in NYC. Demand for affordable housing has vastly outpaced supply, and fewer new units are being constructed because of construction costs. The units available also do not reflect NYC’s household demographics.

New York City households are diverse. One third are singles and 27 percent are families with children.

Data Source: American Community Survey 1-Year Estimates (U.S. Census), 2012.

There is a substantial mismatch between the units available and household size, so that there are not enough studio and 1-bedroom units to house smaller households.

Data Source: Housing and Vacancy Survey (U.S. Census), 2011.

Severely rent burdened households are those paying more than 50% of monthly household income on gross rent. Households paying so much of their income towards rent are at particular risk of housing instability due to the expected wage shock.

Half of the households in NYC have income less than $50,000, and about two-thirds of them have income less than $30,000. Strict testing requirements based on prior earnings increase the number of renters at risk of experiencing housing instability. Without savings, these households will also be vulnerable to falling behind on rental payments.

Sources: American Community Survey (2018), IPUMS USA, NYU Furman Center
